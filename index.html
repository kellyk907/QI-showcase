PDSA Cycles — Three Iterative Rounds

Identify high-risk counties with misalignment score >30. Recruit CHW team. Define SDOH screening protocol for Cycles 1–3.
D

Deploy universal SDOH screening at all primary care touchpoints. Layer care coordination for members with 2+ chronic conditions.
S

Track ED utilization rates monthly. Cycle 1: −4.2%. Cycle 2: −9.1%. Cycle 3: −13.8% cumulative reduction observed.

Standardize CHW referral pathways. Expand transportation voucher program. Revise screening tool based on Cycle 2 gap analysis.

QI Findings — What the Data Showed
01

Geography defines intervention zones. High-risk counties cluster spatially, not randomly — enabling regional care deployment rather than county-by-county fragmentation.

02

Misalignment reveals the hidden gap. 28% of counties show social vulnerability that outpaces clinical burden — populations invisible to diagnosis-only stratification models.

03

Iterative cycles outperform single interventions. Each PDSA cycle refined the referral pathway and reduced variation — moving the process from common-cause to special-cause signal.

04

Uniform strategies fail at scale. National intervention frameworks cannot absorb regional heterogeneity. County-level stratification is a precondition for equitable managed care design.
